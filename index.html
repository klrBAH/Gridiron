Specialization

Offenses in football are focused on scoring a touchdown on almost every drive. Even though every member of the team has a specific role to play, the ball only moves forward if everyone is working in concert towards the same goal. DevOps should inspire the same singular drive to delivering quality software to the end user. Every person on a team should define their success based on their ability to help the team and not just complete a specialized independent task.

Mission Focus

The GridIron Ops framework manifests an easy to understand style of DevOps that focuses the whole team on a single goal: Getting software releases into production. Succeeding at this mission, however, is both challenging and complex. GridIron Ops helps by bringing a “team first” mindset to the game. By highlighting team rather than individual outcomes, the framework keeps teammates focused on the mission and also fosters an engaged and inclusive culture.

Teamwork

DevOps isn’t a tool, or a job, or even a practice. DevOps is a culture. A culture that can best be understood by taking a walk on to the gridiron of American Football. Football is a sport that exemplifies teamwork, built on specialties, but only successful in unity. Booz Allen’s GridIron Ops framework was created to provide a platform to establish a winning DevOps culture in any sized government or commercial entity.

Process

In order to advance the line of scrimmage as a team, everyone needs to know their role in every play. GridIron Ops emphasizes this shared team understanding through its break-down of proven DevOps plays like continuous integration, continuous deployment, automated testing, containerization, and more. Plays are designed to be called as needed, but should also be reviewed and improved after a sluggish deployment. As teams mature, audibles can be used to ensure the team remains flexible enough to defeat any defense standing in the way of a successfully operating deployment.

Culture

Even though coordinating great software development, quality assurance, and technical operations is demanding, DevOps also should be fun. DevOps shouldn’t be scary. Team-oriented DevOps brings fun into the craziness of developing and releasing software. It allows each team member to see their work used by customers in a way that is faster and safer than ever before. It gives engineers in all roles the ability to have an idea and get it out to a user in days instead of months. After all, there’s nothing more fun than seeing your ideas come to life.
